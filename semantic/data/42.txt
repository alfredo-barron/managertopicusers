Effective Use of Naloxone by Law Enforcement in Response to Multiple OpioidOverdoses.
Kitch BB, Portela RC.

 Growing rates of opioid abuse and overdose throughout the nation have lead some community organizations to develop naloxone administration programs.
 In Pitt County North Carolina, two of our law enforcement agencies were trained in the identification of opioid overdose and use of naloxone therapy.
 Attributed partially to introduction of fentanyl into the illicit drugmarket, our community experienced a 48-hour period in which officers successfullydeployed five doses of antagonist medication to four individuals.
 This article presents case descriptions demonstrating the feasibilityand safety of law enforcement naloxone programs.
